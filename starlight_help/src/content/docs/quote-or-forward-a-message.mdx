Conversations in Zulip are [organized by topic](/help/introduction-to-topics),
so it's generally clear what you're responding to when you send a message.
However, when responding to an older message, you may want to
[quote](#quote-a-message) all or part of it for reference.

You can also [forward](#forward-a-message) a message to another conversation.

The first line of a quoted or forwarded message contains a [permanent
link][link-to-message] to the original message. Zulip automatically turns
mentions in the quoted text into [silent
mentions](/help/mention-a-user-or-group#silently-mention-a-user) to avoid
unnecessarily mentioning someone twice.

<ZulipTip>
  You can use [quote blocks](/help/format-a-quote)
  when quoting emails or other non-Zulip content.
</ZulipTip>

## Quote a message

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Desktop/Web">
    <FlattenedSteps>
      1. *(optional)* Select the part of a message you want to quote, or select
         text that spans multiple messages to quote those messages.

      <MessageActionsMenu />

      1. Click the quote option. The label reflects your selection:
         * **Quote message** when there is no selection (or the selection is not
           in this message).
         * **Quote selection** when you selected part of this message.
         * **Quote selected messages** when you selected text across multiple
           messages that include this one.
      1. Send your message.
    </FlattenedSteps>

    <KeyboardTip>
      You can also use <kbd>></kbd> to quote the selected message or text,
      including selections across several messages. Unlike the message menu,
      the keyboard shortcut always follows your current text selection (or the
      focused message if nothing is selected), even if you previously opened the
      menu on a different message.
    </KeyboardTip>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Mobile">
    <FlattenedSteps>
      <MessageLongPressMenu />

      1. Tap **Quote message**.
      1. *(optional)* Delete any parts of the quoted message that are not
         relevant to your reply.
      1. Send your message.
    </FlattenedSteps>

    <ZulipTip>
      If you are in a channel view, you can set a different destination
      topic by tapping the compose box and selecting an existing topic
      or typing a new topic name.
    </ZulipTip>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Forward a message

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Desktop/Web">
    <FlattenedSteps>
      1. *(optional)* Select the part of a message you want to forward, or select
         text that spans multiple messages to forward those messages.

      <MessageActionsMenu />

      1. Click the forward option. The label reflects your selection:
         * **Forward message** when there is no selection (or the selection is not
           in this message).
         * **Forward selection** when you selected part of this message.
         * **Forward selected messages** when you selected text across multiple
           messages that include this one.
      1. Select the desired destination channel or **Direct message** from the dropdown
         in the top left of the compose box.
      1. Enter a topic name, or recipient names for a direct message.
      1. Send your message.
    </FlattenedSteps>

    <KeyboardTip>
      You can also use <kbd>\<</kbd> to forward the selected message or text,
      including selections across several messages. As with quoting, the
      keyboard shortcut always follows your current text selection (or the
      focused message if nothing is selected).
    </KeyboardTip>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
